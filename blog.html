Ethical Hacking

Key psychological factors that contribute to the hacker mindset include: Curiosity and Problem-Solving: Hackers are often driven by an insatiable curiosity to explore the limits of technology. They view systems as puzzles to be solved, and security measures as challenges to overcome.
Social Engineering: Many hackers exploit human psychology to gain unauthorized access to systems. By manipulating social norms and trust, they can trick individuals into revealing sensitive information or granting access to privileged accounts. Anti-Authority Mentality: Some hackers may have a rebellious streak and a desire to challenge established norms and institutions. This can manifest in acts of cybercrime or hacking for social or political causes.
